Hullcracker.io — UX Design Validation Report

_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/ux-designs/ux-Hullcracker.io-2026-07-16/ — DESIGN.md + EXPERIENCE.md

A strong, extraction-ready spine pair: every {path.to.token} reference in both files resolves to a defined DESIGN.md frontmatter token, every color carries a hex, component names are byte-identical across the two files, and both journeys from the decision log land with protagonists, numbered beats, climaxes, and failure paths. The one high-severity rubric gap is inheritance: two adopted GDD E6 information-texture features (the Bounty radar bloom, the Foghorn emote) have no UX treatment anywhere — a story-dev implementing E6 from this contract would have to invent or silently drop them. The remaining rubric findings are medium/low: a handful of visual specs referenced behaviorally but never drawn, the HP Rail spec-vs-mock divergence (honestly flagged, but unresolved), and a miss-splash color left token-less after its old hex was retired.

The three adversarial lenses shift the picture in four ways. First, an arbitration layer is missing: the spec legalizes 10+ simultaneous animated channels with no attention-priority rule, and the primary torpedo warning (the listening-ring pips) is the only threat channel with no pulse grammar at all — economy outshines threat exactly when it matters. Second, dual-coding breaks at blip scale: the flagship Variant C identity system rides on hue alone (a critical accessibility violation of the spec's own floor), and two of four class silhouette features are mathematically subpixel at documented blip sizes. Third, a cluster of contrast repairs is needed — four Regatta hues fail AA as kill-feed text, text-muted fails everywhere it renders, and the storm stroke misses the 3:1 graphics threshold. Fourth, the input review's shipping-blocker was already resolved after review: Eric moved the refit modifier from CTRL to SPACE (hold Space, pick 1–4, release dismisses), which eliminates the uninterceptible CTRL+W close-tab hazard and the CTRL-reach ergonomics cluster; the Sticky-Keys hold-vs-toggle question remains open, since holding Space has the same issue. Post-dedupe, post-Space totals: 3 critical (1 resolved), 17 high (1 resolved), 28 medium (1 resolved), 30 low (1 resolved) — 11 findings need a developer call.

HUD Legibility review (adversarial)

review-hud-legibility.md

Lens: 13-year-old on a 1366×768 low-end Chromebook, 60 FPS, 20-ship match, worst-case screen. "The steady-state frame genuinely honors the guardrail — the composite's squint test passes because it was composed as a calm moment. The spec's failure surface is everything the composite does not show: there is no attention-priority rule among the 10+ animated channels the spec legalizes simultaneously, the floor resolution and both UI-scale options are un-walked (the spec violates its own 9px type floor at 90%), and several blip-scale class claims are mathematically subpixel. All findings are fixable at spec level before implementation; none require abandoning a ratified direction." Counts as filed: 1 critical, 10 high, 11 medium, 3 low.

critical

F1 — No attention-priority hierarchy; the most lethal signal is the least animated

absent from both spines; EXPERIENCE.md · Accessibility Floor / State Patterns
≥10 legal simultaneous animated channels across three corners plus center — and the listening-ring pips, the primary torpedo warning channel, are the only threat channel with no pulse grammar at all. In the worst case every economy/status channel pulses while the thing that kills Marco sits static.
Fix: Add a one-table attention hierarchy (Tier 1 imminent threat > Tier 2 action state > Tier 3 economy); while Tier 1 is active, Tier 3 breathing freezes at its dim keyframe; give close-torpedo pips a Tier 1 pulse. Which channels outrank which is a developer call; the existence of the table is not. Status: OPEN.

Accessibility review (adversarial)

review-accessibility.md

"The accessibility floor is sincerely adopted and unusually well quantified in one place — photosensitivity restraint has real numbers, and the chamfer/silhouette/text dual-coding machinery is genuine design work, not lip service. But two of the seven commitments (colorblind assist, audio-visual redundancy) are promissory notes resting on explicitly undesigned work, and the flagship Regatta/Variant C identity system — the run's biggest new idea — violates the dual-coding floor as written: combatant identity and cross-fog tracking ride on hue alone at blip scale. Fixable before beta, but several load-bearing places currently ship accessibility as intent rather than spec. Verdict: CONDITIONAL PASS — floor accepted in principle, not yet met in spec." Positive findings kept on record: the quantified photosensitivity floor, pre-rolled never-expiring offers as cognitive mitigation, set-and-forget telegraph, cooling-state dual-coding, HP triple-coding, dual settings entry points.

criticaldev call

2.1 — Combatant identity is color-alone at blip scale; the dual-coding floor is violated by the run's flagship system

DESIGN.md · Regatta Hoist; EXPERIENCE.md · Sensor presentation; Journey B beat 3
The blip outline carries class, not identity. Under Variant C — the default — "who is that" and "is that the same hunter" ride entirely on hue. For the ~8% of the male-heavy 10–15 audience with CVD, the tracking feature the spec "embraces" degrades to guesswork; the palette sheet's own CVD notes list four-plus hue collapses. Names appear only in the kill feed — after someone dies, not while tracking. This is precisely what the floor's own words prohibit.
Fix: Pick one or more: (a) nameplate/callsign text on truesight hulls for all combatants; (b) in assist mode, blips gain a 1–2 char identity glyph or numbered pennant; (c) accept Variant P (phosphor-anonymous) as the assist-mode default — cheapest honest option, but must be a stated decision. Status: NEEDS DEVELOPER CALL.
highdev call

1.1 — Colorblind assist is committed but wholly undesigned — and cannot deliver 20 distinguishable identities as worded

EXPERIENCE.md · Accessibility Floor item 4; DESIGN.md · Regatta Hoist
Merged with HUD F17: even the mechanism is undecided ("and/or"); no settings entry, no scope, no acceptance criterion. Under deuteranopia/protanopia ~8–10 distinguishable hues is the practical ceiling for small marks — no swap palette can preserve 20 unique identities; the committed option over-promises. The .working CVD analysis never made it into the spine as requirements.
Fix: Spec now: scope (blips + feed names + hull outlines minimum), mechanism (boosted outlines and reduced-count high-separation palette, with identity degrading to color-family + hover/nameplate confirmation), default off/persisted, acceptance test under simulated deuteranopia at blip scale. Status: NEEDS DEVELOPER CALL on mechanism/re-scope.

3.2 — The listening ring carries no source-type coding: deaf/muted players can't tell torpedo pips from engine pips

EXPERIENCE.md · Accessibility Floor / Listening Ring; DESIGN.md · Listening Ring
Merged with HUD F23: brightness is the only channel and it conflates source-type, loudness, and closeness — a close loud engine produces the same near-white arc as an inbound torpedo, training false positives for everyone and leaving deaf players (and muted school Chromebooks — the primary persona's likely environment) unable to classify the one signal Journey A's lesson depends on.

6.1 — Hold-modifier refit breaks under Sticky Keys / Filter Keys; no toggle alternative is permitted by the logged decision

EXPERIENCE.md · Refit Card ("HOLD, never toggle"); .decision-log.md; input review 2.3
Merged with input 2.3. The CTRL-specific hazard (Sticky-latched CTRL feeding CTRL+W to the browser) is gone with the Space change — but hold-vs-toggle remains open: holding Space has the same Sticky/Filter Keys problem. Fix: Keep HOLD as the default (the design rationale is sound); add a settings option "Refit window: hold / toggle" under accessibility; toggle closes on spend-last, ESC, or re-press; scope-note the decision-log absolute ("hold is the default interaction; toggle exists as an accessibility option"). Input-scheme review (adversarial)

review-input-scheme.md — NOTE: the blocking finding was resolved post-review (refit modifier CTRL → SPACE)

"The core split is sound — tap-telegraph W/S plus hold-rudder A/D on the left, mouse aim/fire on the right, Q/E/R/F within left-hand reach — and the set-and-forget telegraph quietly defuses most chording problems. But the scheme has one shipping-blocking flaw: CTRL as a held game modifier makes CTRL+W (close tab) a designed steady state, and CTRL+W cannot be intercepted by a web page in Chromium browsers — on school Chromebooks a player who taps W while browsing refit cards loses the tab and the match." Post-review resolution (decision log 2026-07-16): Eric approved moving the refit modifier to SPACE (hold Space, pick 1–4, release dismisses; hold-not-toggle unchanged; CTRL leaves the scheme entirely). This resolves the critical finding and its ergonomics cluster; the browser-interception contract reduces to standard hygiene (context-menu suppression, Space-scroll preventDefault). The Sticky-Keys hold-vs-toggle question remains open — holding Space has the same issue.
