Combatant Color System — Three Directions

Every human combatant gets a unique personal color, including you (green=yours is retired). PvE drones render greyscale. Matches hold up to 20 ships, so each direction carries 20 mutually assigned colors, tested here on the true canvas black. Excluding the reserved red, amber, and storm bands leaves ≈250° of hue wheel — all three directions space by lightness as well as hue and lean on the class-coded silhouette channel where hue alone runs out.

#FFB800 amber — reserved: action/warning #7B2FBE storm — reserved: zone red family — reserved: damage/denied #9AA3B2 / #626B78 — drones (greyscale)

Colorblind note — Regatta Hoist

Under deuteranopia/protanopia the yellow-green cluster collapses: Lemon / Chartreuse / Lime / Spring converge toward one dirty yellow, and Green / Jade lose their split. Rose and Periwinkle also drift toward similar grey-blues; Iris sits nearest the reserved storm violet and stays lighter/bluer on purpose — watch it in playtests. Compensation: 20 slots span a wide lightness ladder (Olive vs Lemon, Mulberry vs Magenta survive as light/dark even with hue gone), hulls keep the 0.75px silver contrast stroke, and the class-coded silhouette (Destroyer/Cruiser/Battleship, per GDD) plus text name in feed/results means color is never the only channel.

Colorblind note — Scope Jewels

The cyan-blue-magenta spine survives red-green deficiency best of the three directions, which is why it exists. Remaining risks: Willow vs Citrine and Sea Glass vs Uranium merge under deuteranopia; under the (much rarer) tritanopia the Glacier / Turquoise / Moonstone band and Cerulean / Sapphire pair compress. Amethyst and Tanzanite ride closest to storm violet — both are kept lighter and bluer than #7B2FBE, and the storm ring is a huge screen-scale arc, not a sprite, so scale disambiguates. Compensation: deliberate light/dark alternation between hue neighbors (Beryl vs Malachite, Sapphire vs Lapis), silver hull stroke, and the class-coded silhouette channel.

Colorblind note — Signal Pennants

Honest warning: low chroma carries the least hue signal, so this direction is the riskiest under any color deficiency. Under protanopia/deuteranopia Sage, Dusty Rose, and Mauve all drift toward mid-grey — dangerously close to the drone greys — and Manila vs Celadon, plus the Heather / Dusty Violet / Lilac Ash cluster, can merge. If this register wins, the compensation must be structural, not optional: widen the lightness ladder, shift drone greys darker (≤ #55606B) so no faded pennant ever greys into PvE territory, and treat the class-coded silhouette + silver hull stroke + text-name channels as mandatory. Upside: nothing here can ever be mistaken for amber, storm, or damage red.

TACTICAL-LAYER PROPAGATION

Variant P vs Variant C — same scene, both ways

Deliberately undecided per the decision log: build both as an easy swap and playtest. Illustrated with Direction 1 colors. Hulls carry personal color in BOTH variants — the split is what the radar layer and kill feed do.

Variant P — phosphor-functional

Identity lives on hulls only. Every radar return is anonymous phosphor — a blip is a threat, not a person, until your sight bubble touches it. Kill feed stays neutral scope-text. Cleanest read; preserves radar mystery.

Variant C — personal-color tactical

Blips carry their owner's color and the kill feed reads killer ▸ victim in personal colors, so you can track a rival across the whole match (drone blips stay grey). Trade-off visible above: the Spring-green blip nearly camouflages against sweep phosphor — any green-family personal color pays a tax in this variant.

Easy swap, playtest both. The only differences are (1) blip tint source — constant phosphor vs owner color — and (2) kill-feed name tinting. Both are single lookup points in the renderer (phosphor.ts, killFeed.ts), so the toggle can ship as a dev flag and the winner gets ratified after playtests. Note anti-cheat implication for Variant C: a blip's owner color is identity information leaving the server on radar-only contacts — perception.observe() already decides blip emission, so attaching the owner's palette index adds no positional leak, but it does trade radar anonymity away by design.
