Four Hulls, Four Silhouettes

The current fleet is one chevron scaled three ways — the rendering quality is the keeper, the sameness is not. Below: a genuinely distinct top-down silhouette per launch class, in the same procedural vector-linework language (closed hull polygon, bow-up, code-drawn, silver-accented), shown with the ratified color treatment — bright Regatta Hoist personal color on the OUTLINE, the same hue ~45% value as interior fill; drones greyscale. Silhouettes are gameplay-load-bearing: radar blips carry the hull outline, so class must survive reduction to a dozen pixels. Blip strips at the bottom prove it.

Geometry proposals for reaction — not final art
CLASS 1

Torpedo Boat

“fast, fragile, the needle-threader”

The needle. Extreme length-to-beam (~9:1) — a knife blade with a chevron stern notch. Nothing else on the water is this thin, so at any scale “long and skinny” = torpedo boat, and the shape itself says it threads gaps the others can't. Paired torpedo-tube deck lines amidships are dressing only; the outline carries the identity.

100 u long · 9 u beam — small by area, longest small hull

CLASS 2

Battleship

“massive, heavily armored, long-range artillery”

The fortress. Broadest hull, stepped outline — armor blisters bulge the midships beam outward and step back in, so even the bare outline reads “plated,” not “sleek.” Three turret masses (two forward, one aft, barrels drawn) and a citadel block are interior dressing; the stepped width plus sheer size is what survives to blip scale. Longest hull on the board by rule: the battleship paints bigger.

124 u long · 32 u beam — largest hull, stepped armor line

CLASS 3

Mine Layer

“the trapper”

The stern is the weapon. Hull widens aft — the business end faces backwards — and the broad square stern carries a cut rectangular notch: the laying-rail slot mines roll out of. That rear notch is unique on the board (the torpedo boat's chevron notch is a shallow V; this is a square bite), so “wide back + slot” reads trapper at a glance. Dashed rail line and queued mines are dressing.

88 u long · 20 u beam (widest aft) — mid size, rail notch astern

CLASS 4

Gunboat

“small, fast, lightly armored — speedy boy with some guns”

The compact wedge. Shortest hull, widest for its length (~2.3:1), flared chines, hard flat transom — a stubby arrowhead that looks like it planes on its own wake. Against the torpedo boat (the other small hull) the contrast is pure aspect ratio: dart vs needle. One forward gun mount with a barrel is the “some guns” wink; the wedge outline alone carries the class.

60 u long · 22 u beam — smallest hull, widest ratio

FLEET REVIEW

Relative scale — all four at 1:1

Same pixel scale, side by side. The size rule reads without labels: Battleship largest, Mine Layer mid, Torpedo Boat and Gunboat small — the needle by area, the wedge by length.

BLIP PROOF · VARIANT C

Radar-blip scale — personal colors (preferred default)

Each hull reduced to its outline only, 9–14 px, on the void — the size a radar blip actually paints. Shown upright and rotated, because contacts arrive at every heading. Battleship paints bigger by rule. Drone blip stays grey.

What does the discriminating work at 9–14 px: aspect ratio (needle vs wedge separates the two small hulls at any rotation), overall size (battleship paints visibly bigger), and outline character (the battleship's stepped beam breaks its long edges; the mine layer's square stern bite reads as a blunt, notched end). Interior dressing — turrets, rails, gun mounts — is deliberately absent here: it never ships on the wire, only the outline does.

BLIP PROOF · VARIANT P

Radar-blip scale — single phosphor (playtest swap)

Same strip in anonymous phosphor #7FFFC4 — the Variant P tactical layer, kept as the easy swap. With hue removed, shape and size must carry class alone; this is the harder test and the silhouettes must pass it regardless of which variant wins.

Honest flag for playtest: at 9 px the Gunboat is near the floor of legibility — if it muddies in real phosphor decay, the levers are (a) a slightly larger blip-scale multiplier for the two small classes or (b) exaggerating the wedge's shoulder flare in the blip render. Both are CONFIG-level tweaks, not new art.
