## Overall verdict

A strong, extraction-ready spine pair: every `{path.to.token}` reference in both files resolves to a defined DESIGN.md frontmatter token, every color carries a hex, component names are byte-identical across the two files, and both journeys from the decision log land with protagonists, numbered beats, climaxes, and failure paths. The one high-severity rubric gap is inheritance: two adopted GDD E6 features (the Bounty radar bloom, the Foghorn emote) have no UX treatment anywhere. Remaining rubric findings are medium/low.

The three adversarial lenses shift the picture in four ways. (1) **An arbitration layer is missing:** the spec legalizes 10+ simultaneous animated channels with no attention-priority rule, and the primary torpedo warning — the listening-ring pips — is the only threat channel with no pulse grammar at all. (2) **Dual-coding breaks at blip scale:** the flagship Variant C identity system rides on hue alone (a critical violation of the spec's own accessibility floor), and two of four class silhouette features are mathematically subpixel at documented blip sizes. (3) **A cluster of contrast repairs:** four Regatta hues fail AA as kill-feed text, `text-muted` fails everywhere it renders, the storm stroke misses 3:1. (4) **The Space resolution:** after the input review, Eric moved the refit modifier from CTRL to SPACE (hold Space, pick 1–4, release dismisses) — this resolves the input review's shipping-blocking CTRL+W finding and its ergonomics cluster; the Sticky-Keys hold-vs-toggle question remains open, since holding Space has the same issue.
