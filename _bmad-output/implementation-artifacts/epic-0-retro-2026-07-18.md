## What Went Well

1. **Containment held in all four stories.** The most invasive change the codebase will ever see (a networking-major upgrade) never touched `World`/`Match`; telemetry aggregation stayed pure sim-side; the portal seam is client-only with zero wire changes. The architecture laws (adapter thinness, roster-only schema, zero-Colyseus sim) did exactly what they were written for.
2. **The multi-reviewer adversarial gate (Blind Hunter + Edge Case Hunter + Codex) earned its cost in every story.** All three high-severity catches were beyond unit-test reach:
   - 0.1: rate limit sized for *send* cadence when Colyseus counts *arrival* in 1s windows — honest wifi-stall players would have been force-disconnected (60 → 200).
   - 0.2: punitive kicks (rate-limit 4002, malformed messages) earned reconnection grace, letting a kicked malicious client token back in — confirmed independently by two reviewers.
   - 0.3: the tick-error containment handler itself could throw on a prototype-less error value and kill the whole process — the exact outcome the boundary exists to prevent.
3. **Deferred-work.md discipline.** Nine entries, each carrying evidence and a named natural home (Epic 6.7, Epic 7, "decision for Eric"). This is the project's accountability loop and should continue.
4. **Real-socket smokes as acceptance proof.** reconnectSmoke and metricsSmoke prove the epic's two player-facing promises over actual sockets (pilotless ship sails and is huntable; forged AND replayed tokens rejected; match.end carries all 8 telemetry fields).
5. **Test growth tracked feature risk.** +117 tests concentrated exactly where the epic's risk lived (drop policy matrices, tolerance/abort-once semantics, hostile portal adapters).

## Challenges & Growth Areas

1. **matchSmoke flaked in all four stories.** A/B-proven pre-existing (0/3 clean on the 0.16 baseline) so it never blocked, but it has been "deferred since 0.1" with no owner and no epic home. It is the lifecycle regression signal, and Epic 1 churns the sim across 14 stories. *This is the epic's top carried risk.*
2. **Spec size creep.** Specs 0.2–0.4 all carry `oversized` warnings; 0.2 and 0.3 shipped `followup_review_recommended: true`. Foundation stories are legitimately large, but the trend deserves watching in Epic 1 where stories should be smaller.
3. **The human-pass gap.** 0.1's in-browser end-to-end match and 0.4's "player-identical with the null adapter" playthrough were deliberately left to a human and have not been confirmed done. This is the only Epic 0 acceptance surface resting on construction + headless coverage alone.
4. **Autonomous rulings accumulated without a ratification moment.** Each story shipped "rulings for Eric's morning review"; none have been formally confirmed. This retro is the checkpoint (full list below).

## Key Insights

1. Adversarial multi-model review is the project's highest-leverage quality mechanism for foundation work — keep it for any story touching networking, perception, or lifecycle.
2. "Contained to the adapter layer" is a testable claim (`grep -rn colyseus server/src/game/`), and making constraints grep-able is what made four unattended runs safe.
3. Flaky test infrastructure compounds: every story paid a re-run/triage tax on matchSmoke. Harden shared test infra *before* an epic that leans on it, not during.
4. The deferred-work log converts review noise into a decision queue for Eric instead of losing findings — the retro should sweep it every epic.
