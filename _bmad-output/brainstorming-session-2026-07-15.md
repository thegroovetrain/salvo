## Brainstorming Approach

**Selected Mode:** Guided (refined via Party Mode ×2 + Advanced Elicitation)

**North Star:** Midway between Battleship and World of Warships — WoWS feel without the complexity, preserving Battleship DNA (hidden information, calling shots into the dark; radar sweep as model example). Battleship↔WoWS compass applied at organization time; middle band = buildable zone.

**Session Focus (from Eric) — three open questions:**

1. Class identity: uniform weapon fit works, but open to classes with genuinely different weapons/abilities/uniques (Apex-style kits)
2. Progression: current random-offer upgrade mechanic works, but alternatives welcome
3. Earn model: kills→upgrades vs xp→upgrades — passive XP tick with kills as bonus; key sub-questions: the ratio (how many tick-minutes is a kill worth?) and damage-based vs kill-based bonuses

**Technique Sequence:**

1. Dual-Fantasy Mining — gunnery fantasy AND deduction fantasy; "does this help me shoot, or help me know?"
2. Remix Round (high volume) — steal-and-twist from Apex, WoWS, other BRs, and Battleship (the board game) itself
3. MDA on Class Identity — organize remix material into what Destroyer/Cruiser/Battleship *mean* when kits differ
4. Emergence Engineering + Reward Schedule Architecture + Economy Balancing — kits × upgrades × XP economy as one interacting design space; prompts include "where's the exploit?" and fixed-vs-variable reward timing → Coverage checkpoint: scan neglected dimensions (maps, match structure, meta, social/spectator); bonus technique if needed
5. Emotion Targeting (generative) — the BR match arc; "what does the endgame feel like when X builds collide?"
6. What If Scenarios — wildcards; pre-seeded: "what if damage, not kills, earned the bonus?"

**Capture Conventions:**

- Cost tag (cheap/medium/big) and compass position noted only when instantly obvious — never debated mid-flow; judgment deferred to organization

## Ideas Generated

### Player Fantasies (Dual-Fantasy Mining)

**[Fantasy #1]**: The Needle-Threader — _Core Loop_: line up a torpedo through a terrain gap at an unaware target → travel → payoff. _Novelty_: skill-shots through terrain; islands as offensive tools.

**[Fantasy #2]**: The Narrow Escape — _Core Loop_: detect converging torps → read the geometry → thread the one survivable path. _Novelty_: the helmsman fantasy; kinematics as hero. Dominated all 3 playtests' best moments.

**[Fantasy #3]**: The Dance — _Core Loop_: destroyer orbits and jabs; battleship tracks and swings haymakers. _Novelty_: class asymmetry already generating drama with identical weapons. (Playtest: BB lost both dances — see #15.)

### Sensors & Intel Layer

**[Intel #4]**: Sonar as Third Sensor Tier — radar paints surface; sonar hears below (torps, mines). Cost: medium.

**[Intel #5]**: "Torpedo in the Water" — hydrophone bearing-only warning (direction, not position); escape stays skill-based. Cost: cheap-medium.

**[Intel #6]**: Active Ping — the Flashlight Gamble: reveal a radius, but a larger radius hears YOU. Cost: medium.

**[Intel #7]**: Torpedo Wakes — visible surface tell inside some range. Cost: cheap.

**[Class Kit #8]**: Hydrophones common on all ships; Active Ping class-exclusive (historically the destroyer's). Cost: medium.

**[Class Kit #9]**: Speed Makes You Deaf — hydrophone clarity scales inversely with throttle; welds telegraph to sensors. Cost: cheap.

**[Class Kit #10]**: The Battleship Hears Through Friends — capital ships outsourced ears to escorts; solo-BR translation open (drone escort? worst hydrophones as class weakness?).

**[Intel #19]**: The "Hit!" Call — muffled boom + orange bloom through fog at impact; know you connected, not what/how badly. Cost: cheap.

**[Intel #20]**: Wounded Ships Leak — damaged ships trail smoke above the fog; hurt = trackable, for everyone. Interacts with #16 (does repair douse the smoke?). Cost: cheap-medium.

**[Intel #21]**: Fall-of-Shot Spotting — own splashes visible in fog → bracket-and-walk fire onto radar blips; real-time "B-7". Misses become information. 🧭 Compass-center standout. Cost: medium.

**[Intel #29]**: Ping Warning — being pinged is audible with rough bearing; the flashlight is loud both ways. Cost: cheap.

**[UI #79]**: The Listening Ring — ring at truesight edge with directional pips for everything hydrophones hear. One HUD element carries the whole sonar layer. Cost: medium.

### Counter-Intelligence Suite

**[Class Kit #26]**: Smoke Screen — manufactured fog; vision denial as a verb. Cost: medium.

**[Class Kit #27]**: Radar Scrambler — paint as 5 blips (or none); corrupt enemy deduction. Cost: medium.

**[Kit #69]**: Decoy Buoy — fake ship on radar + engine noise on hydrophones; lies about where you aren't. Cost: medium.

**[World #43-r]**: Fog Banks — the Trade: inside fog your truesight shrinks but you vanish from others' truesight (radar may still paint). Blindness bought with blindness. Cost: medium.

### Class Kits & Identity

**[Class Kit #12]**: Star Shells — battleship fires illumination; combat verb, intel payoff ("shell the darkness"). Cost: medium.

**[Class Kit #13]**: Secondary Battery — auto/low-aim close-radius guns; the orbit tax (historically literate). Cost: medium.

**[Class Kit #14]**: Full Broadside — every gun at once, long reload; timing is the skill. Cost: medium.

**[Class Kit #16]**: Self-Repair on Cooldown — retreat-and-return rhythm; universal vs class-flavored open. ⚖️ Full WoWS damage-control flagged too-WoWS. Cost: cheap-medium.

**[Insight #17]**: Tankiness = Permission to Be Seen — battleship's tank fantasy expressed through the intel system: being spotted doesn't scare it.

**[Class Soul #32]**: Cruiser = The Vulture — reads battles, not water; detects combat signatures far off, tracks wounded ships; the third-party specialist. Cost: medium.

**[Class Soul #33]**: Cruiser = The Duelist — the destroyer-killer counterpick; class triangle edge. Cost: cheap.

**[Class #80]**: The Mine Layer — territory as a class; fourth information relationship: decides where fights may happen. Cost: medium.

### Structural Forks (Class Architecture)

**[Structure #35]**: Classes Are Jobs, Not Hulls — Picket / Torpedo Boat / Sub Hunter / Gunfire Support; jobs have pre-installed fantasies ("balanced" is not a fantasy). (Eric's pivot.)

### Upgrade Economy & Progression

**[Progression #18]**: Build-a-Salvo — ammo stacking already constructs identity mid-match; upgrades may need steering, not replacing.

**[Economy #40]**: Fight First, Then Fruit — tick calibrated so typical player scraps before first point; kills accelerate identity growth.

**[Principle #41]**: The Rat Covenant — hiding is legal and priced (forfeits the power curve); valid as long as it's not the ONLY viable way. (Eric's ruling.)

**[Synthesis #42]**: The Conservation Law — Power Leaks: fighting earns XP but sheds information (flash, smoke, echoes); hiding preserves secrecy but forfeits growth. Self-balancing, no rubber bands. **Session thesis.**

**[Verdict #67]**: Kills Keep the Crown — killing-blow swoop is deliberate risk-reward; damage-small/kill-big hybrid earns a playtest.

**[Economy #82]**: Named Thresholds — 3 stacks in a category = qualitative bonus + a NAME ("Torpedo Boat" on the HUD); builds become earned identities. Cost: medium.

**[Economy #83]**: The Weighted Deck — hull tilts offer odds. Cost: cheap.

**[Economy #84]**: The Rare Pull — small % exotic offers (escort drone, decoy, one-use star shell); variable-ratio thrill, earned never bought. (Eric: "the optimal form of the roguelike style.")

**[Economy #85]**: The Choice-Shaped Deck — picks tilt future offers / unlock deeper category options; spec-in momentum without a tree UI. Cost: cheap-medium.

## Themes and Patterns

1. **Information warfare is the game's true identity axis** — gathering (ping, hydrophones), denying (smoke, fog banks), corrupting (scrambler, decoy), weaponizing (star shells). Most class differentiation ideas differentiate on *knowing*, not shooting.
2. **The Conservation Law** — power leaks: fighting earns strength but sheds information; hiding preserves secrecy but forfeits growth. Self-balancing without rubber bands.
3. **Classes are jobs, not hulls** — jobs have pre-installed fantasies; hull sizes don't. "Balanced" is not a fantasy.
4. **Constitutional pillars adopted:** 10-Minute Covenant, Frantic-to-Play-Light-to-Hold, Rat Covenant, Endgame Guarantee, Paint-Not-Power, Silly Is Sanctioned.
5. **Almost everything reuses the existing spine** — drones, telegraph, perception layer, upgrade offers repeatedly turned out to be half-built foundations for new ideas.
6. **The compass worked** — WoWS-creep vetoes (sectional damage, damage-control parties, elaborate derelicts, torpedo variety) and board-game-creep vetoes (blind storm) kept ideas in the buildable middle band.

## Session Summary

### Most Promising Concepts

**Top Pick: The Conservation Law — Power Leaks (#42, with the information-warfare axis it crowns)**
The session's thesis: fighting earns strength but sheds information (muzzle flash, wound smoke, sonar echoes); hiding preserves secrecy but forfeits growth. It self-balances the BR, honors both the Battleship DNA and the WoWS feel, legitimizes the Rat Covenant, and is enforced by physics the player can see — no rubber bands. Nearly every high-value idea from the session either feeds it or is fed by it.

**Runner-up: Sensors First — the 0.17 Information-Texture Package (#88 + #90)**
Eight cheap, fork-agnostic features (hit call, muzzle flash, wounded smoke, listening ring v1, bounty, endgame tuning, foghorn, mine fuse) that transform the game's information texture without committing to any class architecture. The gunnery-feel trio inside it directly attacks the real playtest finding that fights collapse into truesight range.

**Honorable Mention: Classes Are Jobs, Not Hulls (#35) + the Fork (#86)**
"Picket / Torpedo Boat / Mine Layer / Raider" have souls; "small/medium/large" never will. Whether identity is picked in the lobby (Apex model) or built mid-match through the upgrade deck (roguelike stack #82–85) is the biggest open question — deliberately deferred to a dedicated deep-dive.

### Key Insights

- The dual fantasy is real but asymmetric: playtest joy was kinetic (thread, dodge, dance); the deduction layer is the seasoning that makes kinetic moments feel earned — and it's currently underpaying (blind-fire lands nothing).
- The existing codebase keeps turning out to be half the work: drones, telegraph, perception layer, and the offer system are foundations for most of the top ideas.
- Constitutional pillars now exist to test every future feature against: 10-Minute Covenant, Frantic-to-Play-Light-to-Hold, Rat Covenant, Endgame Guarantee, Paint-Not-Power, Silly Is Sanctioned, storm-stays-damage.
- Counter-intelligence (smoke, scrambler, decoys) is the ownable category claim — but only if the lies are server-authored (#89).
